Economic Forecast Project Economic Outlook Report

7 Frequently Asked Questions

7.1 General Questions

What happened to the static graphs from previous years? We have upgraded our Economic Outlook publication to feature interactive graphs that provide enhanced functionality and deeper data exploration capabilities. The same underlying economic data and analysis remain available, but now with improved visualization tools that allow for customization and dynamic interaction.

Do I need to install any software to use the interactive graphs? No additional software installation is required. The interactive graphs run directly in your web browser using modern web technologies. Simply access our website using a current version of Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge to begin exploring the data.

7.2 Technical Questions

Why are the graphs loading slowly or not displaying properly? Graph loading speeds depend on your internet connection and the complexity of the dataset being displayed. If graphs are not loading properly, try refreshing your browser page or clearing your browser cache. Ensure that JavaScript is enabled in your browser settings, as this is required for the interactive functionality.

The interactive features are not responding when I click or hover. What should I do? This typically indicates a browser compatibility issue or JavaScript being disabled. Verify that you are using a supported browser version and that JavaScript execution is permitted for our website. If problems persist, try accessing the graphs using a different browser or device.

Can I use these graphs on my mobile device? Yes, the interactive graphs are designed to be responsive and function on mobile devices including smartphones and tablets. However, for the most comprehensive experience and full access to all interactive features, we recommend using a desktop or laptop computer with a larger screen.

7.3 Data and Functionality Questions

How do I export or save a customized graph view? Most interactive graphs include export functionality accessible through buttons or menu options within each visualization. You can typically save customized views as PNG images or PDF files, and in many cases, download the underlying data in CSV format for further analysis.

Can I compare multiple economic indicators simultaneously? Yes, many of our interactive graphs support multiple data series display. Look for selection controls, checkboxes, or dropdown menus that allow you to add or remove different economic indicators from the same visualization. This enables direct comparison of trends such as employment rates alongside housing market indicators.

How current is the data displayed in the interactive graphs? The interactive graphs display the same data featured in our annual Economic Outlook publication, which includes the most recent complete year of data along with preliminary estimates for the current year where available. Data sources and collection dates are specified within each visualization and in the accompanying methodology notes.

What if I want to focus on a specific time period or zoom into certain data? Use the zoom functionality by clicking and dragging across the desired time range within any graph. Most visualizations also include time period selection controls that allow you to specify exact date ranges. The reset button will return you to the full dataset view when needed.

7.4 Data Access and Sharing Questions

Can I download the raw data behind the graphs? Yes, most interactive visualizations include data export options that allow you to download the underlying datasets in spreadsheet formats such as CSV or Excel. This enables you to conduct your own analysis or create custom presentations using our economic data.

How should I cite these interactive graphs in my research or presentations? When citing our interactive graphs, reference the Santa Barbara County Economic Outlook publication with the appropriate year, include our research institute name, and specify that data was accessed through our interactive visualization platform. Detailed citation formats are available in the methodology section of our publication.

Can I embed these graphs in my own presentations or website? The interactive graphs are designed for use within our publication platform. For presentations, we recommend using the export functionality to create static images of your customized views. If you need to share interactive functionality with others, direct them to our Economic Outlook publication where they can explore the data themselves.

7.5 Support and Additional Help

Who can I contact if I continue to experience technical difficulties? If you encounter persistent technical issues that prevent you from accessing or using the interactive graphs, please contact our research institute support team. Include details about your browser type and version, operating system, and a description of the specific problem you are experiencing.

Will you provide training sessions on using the new interactive features? We periodically offer webinar sessions demonstrating the capabilities of our interactive graphs and providing tips for effective data exploration. Information about upcoming training opportunities is available through our website announcements and newsletter communications.

How can I provide feedback about the interactive graphs or suggest improvements? We welcome feedback about your experience with the new interactive visualizations. Please contact our research team with your suggestions, comments, or requests for additional functionality. Your input helps us continue improving the accessibility and usefulness of our economic data presentation.

If you encounter any errors, please email us at efp-admin@ucsb.edu.
