Exploring the Economic Trends of Santa Barbara County

Discussion


After a deep decline in median rents between mid-2013 and 2015, rental prices have rebounded and appear to be back on their longer run growth trends. In fact, rental prices in all cities displayed in the following graph have now exceed that of their November 2010 levels. Montecito is the only city where the level of rents have remained depressed as compared to their 2010 values; although they remain far and above the rest of Santa Barbara County in magnitude.

Annual rental price growth has slowed somewhat over the past several years, settling between 1.0% and 3.0% for most cities, with Solvang and Santa Maria experiencing higher rates of growth. The volatility has also declined and rental growth rates appear more sustainable.
